About Us

What We Do

Engineers for a Sustainable World is one of the many project teams on Cornell's campus. Our goal is to create a world of environmental, social, and economic prosperity created and sustained by local and global collective action.

Projects

Most members of ESW participate on one of the four main ESW Projects: Solar Ovens, Human Powered Electricity Generator, Solar Kiosk, and Biofuels. Each of these projects are split into multiple subteams which allow every member to get real design and building experience as an underclassman. We are always open to new ideas for projects; if you have an idea, let us know and we will try to make it happen!

Projects

Education

Every semester we hold a speaker series that brings in professors and industry experts from the Ithaca community to learn about sustainable initiatives in the area. These talks are open to the public so that we can educate the greater population about the work that needs to be done to create a lasting community. We also visit tours of the Ithaca area to highlight the local sustainable practices as well as learn about different types of sustainability.

Outreach

Each year, members of our Solar Cookers and HPEG teams travel to Nicaragua to work with Grupo Fenix, an organization at the Universidad Nacional de Ingeneria in Managua, Nicaragua, and Las Mujeres Solares de Totogalpa, a women's collective in Sabana Grande, Nicaragua. Grupo Fenix works in rural areas to develop, implement and publicize renewable, sustainable solutions to energy needs in rural Nicaragua. Las Mujeres Solares de Totogalpa has worked with Grupo Fenix building and using solar cookers and dryers and has recently opened a Solar Restaurant.
